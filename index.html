About

I like mathematics, philosophy, and health. I am fascinated by algebraic structures, representation theory, and pure mathematics, and curious about mathematics applications, particularly those towards public health and medicine.

I just completed two years as a Post Bachelor Fellow at the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME). At IHME, I use statistically modeling to estimate the global burden of various neoplasms, focusing especially on our estimates of brain tumors, bone cancers, and female specific cancers.

Education

I graduated with a BA in mathematics from Williams College in 2019, where I was also a member of the women's swimming and diving team. After graduating, I started my fellowship with IHME, during which I studied public health, biostatistics, and applied mathematics through courses at the University of Washington. In August 2021, I started a PhD in mathematics at the University of California, Berkeley.
